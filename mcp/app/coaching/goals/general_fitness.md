# Goal module: General fitness — no target race

Consistency beats progression. The plan's job is a sustainable rhythm, not a
peak — there is nothing to peak for.

**Plan shape:**
- Weekly rhythm: mostly easy runs, one slightly longer (60–90 min), strides
  1–2×/week; a light tempo or fartlek only if the athlete *enjoys* it. The
  easy-80% rule (core §3) still applies.
- **Find the maintainable volume — the level absorbed comfortably while life
  happens — and hold it.** Increase only when the athlete wants more, never
  because a plan "should" progress.
- Health metrics are the scoreboard: resting-HR trend, sleep, how runs feel.
  Celebrate consistency streaks, not pace.
- Down weeks still apply: every ~4th week lighter, or opportunistically
  around busy life weeks (watch `life_context` notes).
- **Strength 2×/week matters more here than in race plans** — long-term
  durability *is* the goal (core §8).
- Variety beats staleness: routes, trails, an occasional parkrun; a
  low-stakes event 2–3×/year makes a good rally point.

## Beginner

3 runs/week of 20–40 min, one slightly longer. Progress duration before any
intensity; add strides after a couple of months of consistency.

## Intermediate

3–4 runs/week of 45–75 min (~30–50 km/week). One optional quality day
(fartlek or short tempo). 

## Advanced

5+ runs/week if they love it — treat it as open-ended base training
(Lydiard): generous easy volume, strides, occasional threshold. This athlete
can convert to any race plan (`5k`…`marathon`) on short notice.
