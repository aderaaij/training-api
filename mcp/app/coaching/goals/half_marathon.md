# Goal module: Half marathon

**Plan shape (all levels):** 10–14 weeks (12 typical). **Threshold is king**
— half-marathon race pace ≈ T zone for most athletes, so long-run endurance
and threshold volume are the two pillars; VO2max work is secondary
sharpening. Practice race fueling (drinks/gels) on every long run ≥90 min.
Taper 10–14 days, volume −40–50%, keep intensity.

## Beginner

- 3–4 runs/week, peak ~30–50 km/week. Goal: finish strong.
- Long run 8 → 16–18 km, stepping up ≤2 km at a time with a stepback every
  3rd week.
- Quality: one gentle tempo (15–25 min) per week from mid-plan; strides
  throughout. Nothing else.

## Intermediate

- 4–5 runs/week over ~12 weeks, peak ~50–65 km/week.
- Long run to 19–22 km; on 2–3 of the later ones, finish the last 3–5 km at
  goal half pace.
- 2 quality days: T growing 25 → 40 min (tempo or cruise intervals) + a
  lighter second day (hills early, 800–1000 m I reps later).

## Advanced

- 5–6 runs/week over 12–14 weeks, peak ~65–90 km/week. Pfitzinger-flavored:
- Midweek **medium-long run** 15–19 km (fatigue resistance at a fraction of a
  second long run's cost).
- LT work growing to 6–8 km within a run at ~15K–half pace; long run
  22–26 km with goal-pace segments; I-zone sharpening only in the final
  6 weeks.
