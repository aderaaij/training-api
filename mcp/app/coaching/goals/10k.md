# Goal module: 10K

For an athlete who can run 30+ min continuously and targets a 10K.

**Plan shape (all levels):** 10–12 weeks. The 10K lives between T and I:
**threshold is the highest-return zone** — goal pace is only slightly faster
than T for most athletes. Sharpen with I work in the final month. Taper
~10–14 days, keep intensity.

## Beginner

- 3–4 runs/week, peak ~25–40 km/week. Long run building to 8–10 km.
- Quality progression: strides → fartlek → one gentle tempo (15–20 min at
  "comfortably hard") per week. One quality day maximum.

## Intermediate

- 4–5 runs/week, peak ~40–65 km/week. Long run 10–14 km.
- 2 quality days: T (20–30 min tempo or 3 × 10 min cruise) + I (5–6 × 1000 m
  at 5–10K pace, 2–3 min jog).
- Late plan: goal-pace specificity, e.g. 3 × 2 km at goal pace with 2 min
  recovery.

## Advanced

- 6 runs/week, peak ~65–80 km/week. Long run 14–18 km, optionally with late
  10K-pace segments.
- 2 quality days + strides; alternate T-emphasis and I-emphasis weeks; a
  race-specific block over the final 4 weeks.
