# Goal module: 5K — improve or race

For an athlete who can already run ~30 min continuously and wants a faster or
structured 5K. Starting from zero → use `first_5k` instead.

**Plan shape (all levels):** 8–12 weeks. Anchor paces to a recent race or
time trial (core §2) — 5K race pace sits in the I zone. Progress from general
to specific: strides/hills/threshold early, goal-pace intervals late. Race
week: a short taper of ~7–10 days with reduced volume is enough at this
distance — keep the intensity.

## Beginner

- 3–4 runs/week, peak ~25–40 km/week. Long run 5–8 km easy.
- Quality: strides 2×/week (4–6 × 20–30 s relaxed-fast, full recovery) inside
  easy runs; from mid-plan one fartlek or 6–8 × 400 m at ~5K effort with
  equal-time jog recovery.
- One quality day per week maximum — don't stack tempo and intervals in the
  same week. The goal is a strong, evenly-paced first timed 5K.

## Intermediate

- 4–5 runs/week, peak ~40–65 km/week. Long run 8–12 km easy.
- 2 quality days: one I session (e.g. 5 × 800 m or 6 × 600 m at 5K pace,
  2–3 min jog) + one T session (20 min tempo or 3 × 8 min cruise intervals).
- Final 3 weeks: race-specific work (e.g. 3 × 1 km at goal pace, short rest),
  then the mini-taper.

## Advanced

- 6–7 runs/week, peak ~65–90 km/week. Long run 12–16 km.
- 2–3 quality days: I (1000–1200 m reps at 3–5K pace), T (cruise intervals
  4–5 × 1.6 km or 20–40 min tempo), and early-plan R work (200–400 m at
  ~mile pace, full recovery) for economy.
- Respect the core §5 session caps; add the third quality day only while
  readiness metrics (core §7) stay clean.
